Privacy Policy

Updated: March 30th, 2026

1. Who We Are.

This Privacy Policy describes how The Tech Patron LLC ("we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, stores, and shares your personal information in connection with our products and services, whether provided through our website at thetechpatron.com (the "Site"), in person, by phone, by email, or through any other channel. Depending on the nature of the engagement, The Tech Patron LLC may act either as:

(a) a data controller/business, determining the purposes and means of processing personal information collected directly from you; or
(b) a data processor/service provider, processing personal information on behalf of a client in connection with services provided.

Where The Tech Patron LLC acts as a processor/service provider, it processes personal information solely in accordance with client instructions and applicable law.

2. What Information We Collect.

We may collect the following categories of personal information from you:

(a) Contact Information — including your name, email address, phone number, and mailing address, collected when you engage our services, make a purchase, or otherwise communicate with us.
(b) Payment Information — including credit and debit card details, bank account information for ACH transfers, and billing address. Please note that online payment information is processed directly by Stripe and is not stored by The Tech Patron LLC. Payments made by check or cash are handled directly by The Tech Patron LLC.
(c) Business Information — for B2B clients, we may collect company name, job title, and other relevant business details necessary to fulfill your engagement.
(d) Transaction Information — including details about the products or services you have purchased, order history, and correspondence related to your purchases or engagements.
(e) Technical Information — including basic website usage data collected through functional cookies necessary for the operation of the Site. We do not use tracking cookies or third party analytics tools.
(f) Communications — including emails, phone records, and other correspondence between you and The Tech Patron LLC in connection with your engagement.

3. How We Collect Your Information.

We collect personal information through the following means:

(a) Directly From You — when you contact us by phone, email, or in person to inquire about or purchase our products or services, when you place an order through the Site, when you provide payment information, or when you otherwise communicate with The Tech Patron LLC in connection with an engagement.
(b) Through the Site — when you visit thetechpatron.com, we may collect limited technical information through functional cookies necessary for the operation of the Site. These cookies do not track your activity across other websites and are not used for advertising or analytics purposes. You may disable cookies through your browser settings, however doing so may affect the functionality of the Site.
(c) Through Payment Processing — when you make a payment online, Stripe collects your payment information directly on our behalf. We do not receive or store your full payment details. Please refer to Stripe's Privacy Policy at stripe.com/privacy for more information on how Stripe handles your data.
(d) Through the Course of an Engagement — we may collect additional information from you as necessary to fulfill our services, including technical details about your systems, infrastructure, or business operations shared with us during the course of an engagement.

4. How We Use Your Information.

We use the personal information we collect for the following purposes:

(a) To Fulfill Orders and Provide Services — including processing payments, arranging shipment of physical products, delivering consulting and technical services, and communicating with you about your order or engagement.
(b) To Communicate With You — including responding to inquiries, providing quotes and proposals, sending order confirmations and invoices, and delivering any notices required under our Terms and Conditions.
(c) To Process Payments — including verifying payment information, processing transactions through Stripe or by direct means, and managing billing records.
(d) To Maintain Business Records — including retaining transaction history, engagement records, and correspondence as required for legal, tax, and accounting purposes.
(e) To Improve Our Services — including using general feedback and engagement experience to improve the quality and delivery of our products and services. We do not engage in automated decision-making or profiling that produces legal or similarly significant effects.
(f) To Comply With Legal Obligations — including retaining records as required by applicable law, responding to lawful requests from government authorities, and enforcing our Terms and Conditions.
(g) To Protect Our Rights and Interests — including detecting, investigating, and preventing fraudulent transactions, unauthorized access, and other illegal activities.
(h) We collect only the personal information reasonably necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this Privacy Policy and do not process it in a manner incompatible with those purposes.

5. How We Store and Protect Your Information.

(a) Storage — your personal information is stored securely using Proton's encrypted cloud storage services as our primary storage provider. We may also utilize other reputable cloud storage providers for backup or operational purposes. All storage providers used by The Tech Patron LLC are selected with data security as a primary consideration.
(b) Security Measures — we implement reasonable and appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your personal information against unauthorized access, loss, destruction, alteration, or disclosure. These measures include but are not limited to encrypted storage, secure communication channels, and access controls limiting who within The Tech Patron LLC may access your personal information. Security measures are designed to be appropriate to the nature and sensitivity of the information processed and may include encryption, access controls, authentication mechanisms, and monitoring systems.
(c) Payment Security — online payments are processed exclusively through Stripe, which maintains its own security standards including PCI DSS compliance. The Tech Patron LLC does not store full payment card details. For payments made by check or cash, physical records are stored securely and access is limited to authorized personnel only.
(d) Data Retention — We retain personal information only as long as necessary for:

(i) providing services;
(ii) maintaining business and legal records; and
(iii) complying with applicable law.

Retention periods may vary depending on the nature of the information and legal requirements. In general, we retain transaction and engagement records for up to seven (7) years unless a longer period is required or justified.
(e) Limitations — while we take the protection of your personal information seriously, no method of electronic storage or transmission is completely secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security of your information and encourage you to take appropriate steps to protect your own data when communicating with us online.

6. Who We Share Your Information With.

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties. We may share your personal information only in the following limited circumstances:

(a) Payment Processors — when you make a payment online, your payment information is shared with Stripe solely for the purpose of processing your transaction. Stripe's use of your information is governed by their own Privacy Policy available at stripe.com/privacy.
(b) Shipping Carriers — when you purchase physical products that require shipment, we share your name, mailing address, and order details with our shipping carriers, including but not limited to UPS, FedEx, and USPS, solely for the purpose of fulfilling your delivery.
(c) Cloud Storage Providers — your information may be stored on servers maintained by our cloud storage providers, including Proton as our primary provider and other reputable providers used for backup or operational purposes. These providers are prohibited from using your information for any purpose other than providing storage services to The Tech Patron LLC.
(d) Legal and Regulatory Authorities — we may disclose your personal information to government authorities, law enforcement agencies, or other third parties where required to do so by applicable law, court order, or regulation, or where we believe such disclosure is necessary to protect our rights, your safety, or the safety of others.
(e) Business Transfers — in the event of a merger, acquisition, reorganization, or sale of all or substantially all of the assets of The Tech Patron LLC, your personal information may be transferred to the acquiring entity as part of that transaction. We will notify you of any such change in accordance with the Notices provisions of our Terms and Conditions. Any successor entity will be required to honor the commitments set forth in this Privacy Policy.
(f) With Your Consent — we may share your personal information with other third parties where you have provided your explicit consent for us to do so.

We are not responsible for the privacy or data security practices of third-party providers, including payment processors, cloud providers, or software vendors, which are governed by their own policies.

7. Your Rights Regarding Your Personal Information.

(a) General Rights — regardless of your location, you have the following rights with respect to your personal information held by The Tech Patron LLC: Right to Access — you have the right to request a copy of the personal information we hold about you. Right to Correction — you have the right to request that we correct any inaccurate or incomplete personal information we hold about you. Right to Deletion — you have the right to request that we delete your personal information, subject to our legal obligations to retain certain records as described in Section 5(d). Right to Object — you have the right to object to our use of your personal information in certain circumstances. Right to Restrict Processing — you have the right to request that we limit the way we use your personal information in certain circumstances.
(b) California Residents — CCPA Rights — if you are a resident of California, you have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act ("CCPA"), including:

(i) The right to know what personal information we collect, use, disclose, and sell about you.
(ii) The right to request deletion of your personal information, subject to certain exceptions.
(iii) The right to opt out of the sale of your personal information.
(iv) The right not to be discriminated against for exercising your CCPA rights.

We do not “sell” or “share” personal information as defined under California law. We do not use personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising. Also, California residents may designate an authorized agent to submit requests on their behalf, subject to verification requirements. To exercise your CCPA rights, please contact us using the information provided in Section 1 of this Privacy Policy.
(c) European Union Residents — GDPR Rights — if you are a resident of the European Union, you have additional rights under the General Data Protection Regulation ("GDPR"), including: The right to data portability — you may request that we provide your personal information in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format. The right to withdraw consent — where our processing of your personal information is based on your consent, you have the right to withdraw that consent at any time. The right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority if you believe we have not handled your personal information in accordance with applicable law. The legal bases on which we process your personal information include the performance of a contract with you, compliance with legal obligations, and our legitimate business interests in operating and improving our services. We process personal information under the following legal bases:

(i) Contractual necessity — to perform services you request
(ii) Legitimate interests — to operate, improve, and secure our services
(iii) Legal obligations — to comply with applicable law
(iv) Consent — where required (e.g., certain communications)

(d) How to Exercise Your Rights — to exercise any of the rights described in this Section, please contact us at the email address or mailing address provided in Section 1 of this Privacy Policy. We will respond to your request within a reasonable timeframe and in accordance with applicable law. We may require verification of your identity before processing requests, including matching identifying information or requesting additional documentation where necessary.
If we deny your request, you may have the right to appeal our decision by contacting us using the information in Section 1. We will respond in accordance with applicable law.

8. Cookies.

(a) What Are Cookies — cookies are small text files placed on your device by a website when you visit it. They are widely used to make websites function properly and to provide a better browsing experience.
(b) How We Use Cookies — The Tech Patron LLC uses only functional cookies that are strictly necessary for the operation of the Site. These cookies enable core website functionality such as page navigation, session management, and security. We do not use cookies for advertising, tracking, or analytics purposes, and we do not share cookie data with any third parties.
(c) Types of Cookies We Use — the cookies used on the Site are limited to the following: Session Cookies — temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser. These are used to maintain your session while you navigate the Site. Security Cookies — used to authenticate users and prevent fraudulent use of the Site.
(d) Managing Cookies — you may control or disable cookies through your browser settings at any time. Please note that disabling cookies may affect the functionality of the Site and your ability to access certain features. Instructions for managing cookies can typically be found in your browser's help documentation.
(e) Third Party Cookies — we do not permit third parties to place cookies on the Site. Any third party services linked to or referenced on the Site, such as Stripe for payment processing, may use their own cookies in accordance with their own privacy policies. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any third party services you interact with.
(f) Our Site does not respond to “Do Not Track” signals due to the absence of tracking or advertising technologies.
